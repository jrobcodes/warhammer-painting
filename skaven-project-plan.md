## Paint Notes

### Color Scheme Considerations
Skaven are flexible — no official "chapter colors" like Space Marines. Common approaches:
- **Clan-based**: Brown/green for Pestilens, brass/green for Skryre, black for Eshin, grey/brown for Verminus
- **Unified army**: Pick a unifying cloth color (dirty red, off-white, sickly green) across all units
- **Grimdark/filthy**: Skaven practically beg for weathering, rust, grime, and Nurgle-adjacent grossness

### Recommended Techniques
- **Fur**: Drybrush-heavy approach works great. Base dark brown, drybrush lighter browns, edge with bone
- **Warpstone**: Warpstone Glow + Moot Green highlight for the classic toxic green glow
- **Metals**: Leadbelcher base, Agrax Earthshade, Typhus Corrosion + Ryza Rust for corroded weapons
- **Plague Monks**: Lean into pale/sickly skin, tattered green robes, and rust
- **Bases**: Mud, rubble, sewer gratings, toxic puddles (Nurgle's Rot technical paint)

### Batch Painting Suggestion
With ~210 Citadel paints, you have everything you need. For the 40+ Clanrats and 20 Plague Monks, batch painting in groups of 10 is recommended. Skaven reward speed painting — they're a horde army and look best en masse rather than with individual showcase quality.

## Honest Assessment

Your current Skaven collection (1,280 pts from two box sets) is in a good spot:

- **500pts**: Fully playable right now with no purchases
- **1,000pts**: Playable at 940pts with owned models — close enough for casual games
- **2,000pts**: Requires ~$185 in additional purchases, which is reasonable for a full army

For a secondary/tertiary painting army, this is a solid foundation. The two big war machines (Screaming Bell + Plague Furnace) are excellent centerpiece painting projects, and the horde infantry rewards batch painting techniques. The endless spells are fun painting side projects.

If you decide Skaven stays purely a painting project and you never expand to 2,000pts, you still have a perfectly functional ~1,000pt force for casual games — and every model is worth painting on its own merits.
