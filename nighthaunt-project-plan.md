## Paint Scheme Suggestions

Nighthaunt are one of the most painter-friendly armies in the game. The ethereal models respond incredibly well to drybrushing, Contrast paints, and simple techniques that produce dramatic results.

### Classic Nighthaunt (Spectral Green-White)
The "box art" scheme — fast and effective:
- **Prime**: Corax White or Grey Seer spray
- **Robes/Ethereal parts**: Nighthaunt Gloom (Contrast) over white primer — one coat does most of the work
- **Alternative**: Hexwraith Flame (Technical) for a greener, more intense ghost effect
- **Drybrush up**: Ulthuan Grey, then White Scar on the highest points
- **Weapons/Chains**: Leadbelcher, Nuln Oil wash, Ironbreaker drybrush
- **Bases**: Astrogranite Debris texture paint, Dawnstone drybrush, tufts of dead grass

### Icy Blue Ghosts
A colder, more ethereal look:
- **Prime**: Grey Seer
- **Robes**: Aethermatic Blue (Contrast) — gives a pale ice-blue
- **Shade recesses**: Drakenhof Nightshade
- **Drybrush**: Ulthuan Grey, White Scar
- **Weapons**: Same metallics as above

### Crimson Spirits (Shyish Death Magic)
For a more sinister, bloody look:
- **Prime**: Wraithbone
- **Robes**: Flesh Tearers Red (Contrast) thinned with Contrast Medium
- **Drybrush up**: Screamer Pink, Pink Horror on edges
- **Weapons**: Retributor Armour with Agrax wash for corroded gold

### Painting Resources
- **Warhammer TV** "How to Paint Nighthaunt" series on YouTube — covers exactly these Tempest of Souls models
- **Squidmar Miniatures** did a spectacular Nighthaunt speed-paint video
- **Juan Hidalgo** (YouTube) has an excellent ethereal glow tutorial
- Nighthaunt are ideal "speed paint" models — you can get a full unit done in an evening with Contrast paints and drybrushing
