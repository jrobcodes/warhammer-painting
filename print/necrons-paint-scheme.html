Szarekhan Necrons — Paint Scheme

Duncan Rhodes method • Step-by-step with placement guide • All paints owned

Warrior Scheme

For: Warriors, Skorpekh Destroyers, Royal Warden, Scarabs, Lokhust Heavy Destroyers, Cryptothralls, Reanimator, Immortals, Wraiths, Triarch Stalker

Step 1 — Prime & Darken
1
Leadbelcher Spray Citadel Spray
Spray the entire model evenly. This is your primer + base coat in one.
Or: prime black first, then airbrush Stynylrez metallic as zenithal from above.
2
Black Templar Citadel Contrast
Apply over the entire model. Pools into recesses to create depth. Don't let it pool on flat surfaces — wick away excess with a clean brush.
3
Nuln Oil Citadel Shade
Wash into recesses only — joint lines, panel gaps, between ribs. Not all-over.
This deepens the shadow lines that Black Templar started.
Step 2 — Bronze / Brass Trim
4
Runelord Brass Citadel Base
Paint raised trim, shoulder pads, chest symbol, spine ridge, kneecaps. The Szarekhan dynasty markings — the decorative edges and borders on the armor.
5
Canoptek Alloy Citadel Layer
Highlight the top edges of all bronze areas — upper rim of shoulder pads, top of chest symbol, raised edges of trim. Leave Runelord Brass visible in lower areas.
6
Cryptek Armourshade Citadel Shade
Wash into the recesses of bronze areas only — where trim meets armor panels, around rivets on shoulder pads. Deepens the brass tones.
Step 3 — Silver Edge Highlights
7
Stormhost Silver Citadel Layer
Fine edge highlight on the sharpest edges of the dark metallic body — edges of leg plates, arm ridges, head crest, ribcage edges. Small dots on rivets. Keep it subtle.
Step 4 — Green Gauss Glow
8
Matt Black Army Painter
Base coat the gun barrel interior, eyes, chest orb — anywhere the green glow will go. This creates a dark base for the glow to pop against.
9
Caliban Green Citadel Base
Paint the entire glow area — gun barrel, gauss rod, eyes, chest orb. Leave a thin black ring around the edges.
10
Warpstone Glow Citadel Layer
Layer on the center of each glow area, leaving Caliban Green visible at the edges. On the gauss rod, paint the center line.
11
Moot Green Citadel Layer
Thin line/dot at the very center of glow points — center of eyes, tip of gauss rod, brightest spot of chest orb. This is the "hottest" point of the glow.
12
Mechanicus Standard Grey Citadel Base
Mix with Moot Green for a desaturated green-grey. Apply as an OSL (object source lighting) haze on the metal surfaces near glow sources — around eyes, under gun barrel.
Optional but adds realism. Thin this down, it should be barely visible.
13
Matt White Army Painter
Tiny dot of white at the absolute brightest point of each eye/orb. Creates the "pinprick" light effect.
14
Biel-tan Green Citadel Shade
Glaze over all green glow areas to unify the layers and add a glossy, ethereal look. Also apply to the gauss rod to tie the green tones together.
15
Casandora Yellow Citadel Shade
Tiny touch on the very center of the hottest glow points (center of eyes). Shifts the brightest spot from green towards yellow-white, mimicking intense energy.
Less is more — one small dab per eye.
Step 5 — Basing
16
Moon Dust Army Painter
Apply to base — Martian/desert theme for Szarekhan tomb world.

Overlord Scheme — Additional Steps

For: Overlord(s), Skorpekh Lord, Plasmancer, Technomancer, Psychomancer. Do the full Warrior scheme first, then add these.

Gold Rank Details (after Warrior step 7, before green glow)
A1
Retributor Armour Citadel Base
Paint gold rank markings — Overlord's face plate, crown/crest, chest eagle, weapon handle, ornamental details. On Skorpekh Lord: blade guards, spine crest.
Gold goes on top of (replaces) the dark metallic in these areas. More gold = higher rank.
A2
Liberator Gold Citadel Layer
Highlight the upper edges of gold areas — top of crown, raised face plate ridges, weapon pommel edges. Brighter gold over the darker Retributor base.
Robes & Cloth (Overlord only)
A3
Wyldwood Citadel Contrast
Apply over the tabard/loincloth/hanging cloth on the Overlord. Let it pool in folds — Contrast does the shading for you.
A4
Basilicanum Grey Citadel Contrast
Apply to any secondary cloth or leather straps — belt, arm wraps, underside of shoulder cape if present.
Additional Metallic Detail
A5
Iron Hands Steel Citadel Base
Paint blade edges, weapon blades, sharp metallic details that should look like polished steel (different tone from the body's darker Leadbelcher).
A6
Cryptek Armourshade Gloss Citadel Shade
Wash over gold areas and blades. The gloss finish makes metals look polished and rich. Apply thinly — don't let it pool on flat gold surfaces.
Final Highlights
A7
Corax White Citadel Base
Mix with Administratum Grey for cloth highlights — top of cloth folds on the tabard, highest points of draped fabric.
A8
Administratum Grey Citadel Layer
Edge highlight on Basilicanum Grey cloth/leather areas — top edges of straps, raised folds.
Lychguard — Extra Gold: Follow Warrior scheme for the body, then apply Retributor Armour + Liberator Gold much more generously — cover shield faces, entire shoulder plates, chest plate, and helmet (not just trim). These are elite bodyguards; more gold = higher rank. Wash gold with Cryptek Armourshade Gloss.
C'tan Void Dragon: Custom scheme. Dark metallic body (Warrior steps 1-3), then heavy green energy effects across the energy tendrils, chest core, eyes, and base lightning. Use the full green glow sequence (steps 8-15) but more aggressively — bigger glow areas, more white/yellow at the hottest points. Consider Vallejo Fluorescent Green for extra pop.
Scarab Swarms (speed method): Prime black, heavy drybrush Leadbelcher over the whole base, Nuln Oil wash, then pick out a few wing edges with Stormhost Silver and eyes with Moot Green. Done — don't overthink these, they're tiny.
