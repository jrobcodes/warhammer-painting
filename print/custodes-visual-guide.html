Recommended Batch Painting Order

Paint models in batches — do the same step on all models before moving to the next step. This is MUCH faster than painting one model at a time.

Phase 1Prime + Base Gold — Spray/brush Retributor Armour on Shield-Captain, Guard x4, Praetors x3 (8 models at once). Prime Prosecutors BLACK separately.
Phase 2Shade Gold — Reikland Fleshshade wash on all 8 gold models. Let dry completely (30 min). While drying, base coat Prosecutor armor with Leadbelcher.
Phase 3Red — Khorne Red on all plumes, capes, tabards across all gold models. 2 thin coats.
Phase 4Black details — Abaddon Black on joints, weapon hafts, undersuits on all gold models.
Phase 5Gold highlights — Liberator Gold on raised edges of all gold models. Then shade red with Nuln Oil.
Phase 6Final highlights — Stormhost Silver edges on gold. Evil Sunz Scarlet highlights on red. Eshin Grey edges on black.
Phase 7Prosecutors — Nuln Oil wash on silver, then gold trim, cloth, skin. Save faces for last.
Phase 8Weapon blades — Leadbelcher + Nuln Oil + Stormhost Silver on all weapon blades across the army.
Phase 9Gems + details — Caliban Green + white dot on all gems. Quick and fun.
Phase 10Bases — Texture paint all 13 bases at once. Drybrush. Add tufts. DONE!

Beginner Tips for First-Time Painters

Welcome to the hobby, Darren! These tips will save you time and frustration.

1. Thin Your Paints The #1 rule. Add a small drop of water to your paint on a palette (a paper plate works). You want the consistency of milk, not toothpaste. Thick paint hides detail and looks lumpy.
2. Two Thin Coats One thin coat often looks patchy — that is normal! Let it dry completely (5-10 minutes), then apply a second thin coat. Two thin coats always looks better than one thick coat.
3. Let Each Layer Dry Seriously. Wait until the previous layer is fully dry before painting over it. Painting on wet paint = muddy mess. Go make a cup of tea between coats.
4. Shake Your Paints Shake each pot vigorously for 30 seconds before opening. The pigment settles to the bottom. If you skip this, you will get watery paint with no color.
5. Use a Wet Palette Ask jrob to show you his wet palette. It keeps paint from drying out on your workspace. A DIY version: damp paper towel in a tupperware lid, parchment paper on top.
6. Don't Panic About Mistakes Made a mistake? Just wait for it to dry, then paint over it with the correct color. Acrylic paint is very forgiving. Everyone makes mistakes, even experts.
7. Wash = Instant Shadows Shade/wash paints (like Reikland Fleshshade and Nuln Oil) are your secret weapon. They flow into recesses and create shadows automatically. One coat of wash makes a model look 10x better.
8. Edge Highlighting for Beginners Use the SIDE of your brush, not the tip. Lightly drag the side of the brush along raised edges. The paint catches on the edge naturally. Practice on a spare bit of sprue first.
9. Clean Your Brush Often Rinse your brush in water every few minutes. Don't let paint dry in the bristles — it ruins brushes fast. Always re-wet before picking up more paint.
10. Your First Models Won't Be Perfect And that is completely fine! Every painter's first model looks rough. By model #5 you will already see improvement. The goal is "table ready" — looks good from arm's length.
The good news about Custodes: They are one of the EASIEST armies to paint because gold spray does 70% of the work. You have only 13 models to paint (most armies have 30-100+). And the gold scheme is very forgiving — Reikland Fleshshade wash covers up a lot of imperfections.

Essential Supplies Checklist

BrushesAt minimum: 1 medium brush (basecoating), 1 small brush (details), 1 drybrush. Ask jrob to lend spares.
Water cupAny mug or cup. Fill with clean water. Change it when it gets murky.
Paper towelsFor wiping excess paint off your brush. You will use a lot.
PalettePaper plate, old tile, or wet palette. Somewhere to put paint before it goes on the model.
Hobby knifeFor cleaning mold lines off models before priming. Scrape gently along visible lines.
Plastic glueFor assembling models. Citadel Plastic Glue or Tamiya Extra Thin Cement.
Primer sprayGold (Retributor Armour) for Custodians. Black for Prosecutors. Spray outside in 50-80F weather.
Where to paint: Find a well-lit spot with a table you don't mind getting paint on. Good lighting is crucial — you can't paint what you can't see. A desk lamp angled at your model makes a big difference.
